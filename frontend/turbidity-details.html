    Why Turbidity Matters

    Turbidity measures how cloudy or hazy water is due to suspended particles. High turbidity can indicate contamination from soil, waste, or microorganisms. According to WHO guidelines, drinking water should have turbidity less than 5 NTU. Clear water is not only safer to drink but also more effective for disinfection processes like chlorination.
